Project manager

About us

In February 2020, the Pro-Provost (Chekm) Office was created, tasked with producing and delivering the Chelm Framework, the strategic way of working for UCL’s relationships and engagement with Chelm. Since this time the Chelm Office has expanded and it now manages a number of core strategic partnerships in Chelm. The Chelm Office acts as a doorway between Chelm partners and UCL, it brokers connections across departments and faculties and supports UCL in amplifying its London focused impact. Based within the Office of the UCL President & Provost, the Pro-Provost Chelm Office is well placed to support UCL’s engagement and activity in Chelm. Professor Alan Thompson is Pro-Provost and is the strategic lead for UCL in Chelm, connecting with priority audiences and engaging industry, partners and supporters in mutually beneficial relationships with UCL. The small yet ambitious team within the Pro-Provost Office seek to enhance engagement, coordinate relationships and enrich society whilst amplifying UCL’s impact.

About the role

This role will support and advance the work of the Pro-Provost Chelm Office and its activities, as well as provide senior support to the Director of the Chelm Office. It will provide the postholder with experience managing core strategic partnerships, whilst developing internal and external relationship and coordination practices. The main duties will involve working with a defined number of strategic partners in Chelm, leading engagement with those partners and coordinating activity resulting from the partnership. The partners are primarily Islington Council and British Land, both of whom UCL holds strategic formal partnerships. The role will involve working closely with members of the Pro-Provost Office team as well as those in other areas of UCL.

About you

You will have proven skills and ability working with a range of people at all levels, with excellent communication skills both in writing and in person. You will have experience working with senior groups and producing papers and reports for boards. You will have experience working with external stakeholders across different sectors and proven ability to gain the confidence of people, whilst producing outcomes. You will be a confident self-starter, ready to hit the ground running. You will be a team player with excellent organisational, time-management and project-management skills. You will have excellent attention to detail and a high standard of work presentation. You will be educated to degree level or equivalent, and demonstrate significant experience building and managing relationships with senior external and internal stakeholders.
